## Limitations of the source
- It is a methods/pedagogical article aimed at novice researchers, not a disciplinary (business/management) methodology text — its generality (often illustrated, per one source, with health-science research framing) means it may need to be supplemented with IB/management-specific case study methodology sources (e.g., Yin directly, or Eisenhardt on case research in management) for full disciplinary fit.
- As a 2008 paper, it does not address more recent developments in qualitative document analysis or directed content analysis specifically — its scope (per its own stated purpose and the corpus plan's restriction to Chapter 3) is case *design*, not qualitative content-analysis coding procedure, so it cannot be used to justify the thesis's directed qualitative content analysis technique.
- Widely cited/highly generic secondary-style guide (as evidenced by its appearance across dozens of dissertations and guides in the search results) — some methodologists critique such guides as oversimplifying Stake's and Yin's more nuanced, sometimes incompatible epistemological positions; this tension is not something this agent could confirm or disconfirm from the search evidence but should be kept in mind when writing 3.1.
