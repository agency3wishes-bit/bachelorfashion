## H&M Group 2022

**Document:** H&M Group Sustainability Disclosure 2022 (90 pp.), reporting period 1 Dec 2021 – 30 Nov 2022. File: data/reports/HM_Group_Sustainability_Disclosure_2022.pdf

**Coding notes (page offsets, methodology, gaps):**

PAGE OFFSET: This is a single, non-split PDF. Verified directly on first read: the printed footer on every page reads "P. N ... LEADING SUSTAINABILITY REPORTING SINCE 2002" and N matches the PDF page number exactly (checked PDF pp.2,3,4,5,10,20,26-31 etc.). PDF page = printed page = internal page throughout. No offset needed or recorded for this file (unlike the Inditex multi-part splits).

METHOD: Used pdftotext -layout page-by-page, cross-checked against raw (non-layout) extraction for two-column pages where -layout merged text out of order (p.28: "dropped to 701 (91 in 2021)" is a layout-merge artefact — raw extraction confirms it is "dropped to 70[footnote-marker 1] (91 in 2021)", i.e. 70, not 701; similarly p.29's "+186"/"+326" % figures are the real percentages (+18%, +32%) with a superscript footnote digit (6) merged onto the end by -layout — verified against the narrative text on p.28 which states the absolute tonnage change and allows the % to be recomputed independently). Coders should re-verify layout-mode tables against raw or narrative text before quoting any figure that looks anomalously large.

TOC located on p.3 and used to navigate directly to each theme (Climate p.26, Biodiversity p.32, Water p.35, Chemicals p.38, Moving to a circular ecosystem/Material choice/Microfibres pp.40-48, Resource optimisation & recirculation p.49-51, Circular customer journey pp.53-56, Fair & Equal overview p.61, Social impact in own operations p.65-66, Social impact in production supply chain pp.67-72, Inclusion & diversity p.77, Community engagement p.81, Supply chain management p.82). Transparency section (pp.13-14, under "How we lead change") was also read in full and used for the Consumers/end-users theme because it contains the report's own disclosure of external criticism of its "Conscious" product label and a large self-reported decline in stakeholder-rated transparency trust — directly on-topic for RQ2.

No pilot rows (P1-P12) cover H&M 2022 (the pilot used H&M's FY2024 report), so all 30 rows below are newly coded, none folded from the pilot.

ANTICIPATORY COERCIVE PRESSURE (task-flagged item): confirmed present and distinctive to this pre-CSRD report. Multiple units (climate p.16/26, chemicals p.38, waste p.50, consumers p.13-14) frame CSRD/CSDDD/EU Taxonomy/EPR/Green-Claims-type "emerging legislation" as something H&M is "supporting development of" or "preparing to comply with," i.e. anticipatory/agenda-shaping language rather than the "we comply with CSRD/ESRS" framing expected in the 2023-2025 reports. Recorded as a cross-theme pattern (rows C1, Ch3, Wa3, Cu1) for the Ch.4.4 cross-year comparison.

GOVERNANCE: searched for executive-pay-linked-to-sustainability-KPI disclosure; found no explicit unit in this report tying board/executive remuneration to sustainability metrics (the only remuneration content found, p.65-66, concerns general employee Compensation & Benefits Policy and the H&M Incentive Program share-option scheme for all employees, not executive pay). Not coded as a separate theme per the task's instruction to skip if genuinely absent.

CROSS-YEAR NOTE FOR STAGE 10: full-text search of this document for "turnover," "% of sales," "share of revenue," "percent of revenue" returned zero matches — the 2022 report does not disclose a turnover-share denominator for its circular-customer-journey (resell/rental/repair) initiatives, unlike the FY2024 report (pilot row P4: resell = 0.6% of turnover). This suggests the turnover-denominator disclosure practice for this specific metric was introduced between 2022 and 2024 — worth confirming against the 2023 report at Stage 10's cross-year stage before treating it as a settled trend from only two data points.
