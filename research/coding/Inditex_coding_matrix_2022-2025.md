## Inditex 2022

Report structure: 5.1 Our people (own workforce) pp.147-180ish (part2); 5.2 Our customers (consumers) pp.181-184 (part3); 5.3 Our products (materials/circularity) pp.185-194 (part3); 5.4 Our shareholders pp.195-196 (not coded, out of theme scope); 5.5 Environment pp.197-213 (part3) with sub-sections 5.5.1 energy/climate (197-205), 5.5.2 water (206-208), 5.5.3 biodiversity (209-211), 5.5.4 waste (211-213) — note chemicals/pollution has NO standalone numbered sub-section in this pre-ESRS report; chemicals content is folded into 5.5.1 (energy/supply-chain chemical management, ZDHC), 5.5.2 (Care for Water chemical-use benchmarking) and a short atmospheric-emissions/noise sidebar (p.205); 5.6 Suppliers (workers in value chain) pp.214-236 (part3), including 5.6.2 "Workers at the Centre" strategy and its "Living Wages" sub-section (p.227); 5.7 Communities pp.237-256 (part3, spanning into part4's offset boundary at printed 257 = Governance start). Governance (p.257+) was not coded per the Stage 10 brief except one adjacent item folded into Own Workforce (sustainability-linked variable remuneration for office staff, p.171) because it is directly relevant to a disclosure-quality/greenwashing question, per the brief's carve-out.

Before/after comparison (as requested): this 2022 report is structurally looser and less standardised than the ESRS-structured Inditex 2024 report referenced in the pilot (`pilot_coding_report.md`). Concretely: (1) no ESRS topical-standard cross-references (E1/E5/S1/S2 tags) anywhere in this report — sub-topics are organised under the company's own "Committed to" headings rather than a mandated taxonomy; (2) Chemicals/Pollution has no dedicated numbered sub-section at all in 2022 (unlike the 2024 report, which the pilot's page map treats as ESRS E1/E5-anchored with a chemicals dimension inside E5) — it had to be reconstructed from scattered water/energy passages; (3) the 2022 "Living Wages" sub-section (p.227) discloses ZERO outcome or reach metrics (not even a people-reached figure), which is thinner than the 2024 report's pilot row P11 (86,268 people reached for the same-named PIA) — i.e., the disclosure gap the pilot flagged as a "candidate, pending a second year to confirm a pattern" is shown by this earlier 2022 report to already have existed at least one full reporting cycle before the pilot's 2024 sample, strengthening (not just replicating) that candidate finding; (4) the materials section's headline metric terminology itself changed across years within the same company — this 2022 report's own footnote 20 (p.188) states "we have aligned the terminology used in our objectives with that currently being used by benchmark industry organisations such as Textile Exchange. The meaning and ambition of our goals remains unchanged" — a self-acknowledged terminology shift between "preferred raw materials" (2022, this report) and "lower-impact fibres" (2024, per pilot P9), asserted without a demonstrated conversion/comparability check.

Golden rule applied throughout: several passages read as promotional (e.g., "we achieved our goal of 100% renewable electricity," "we are firmly committed to...") but were NOT given a greenwashing-indicator code unless a concrete textual basis (a comparable cross-referenced figure, an absent denominator elsewhere disclosed for analogous metrics, an explicit scope gap, etc.) was identified; several rows below therefore carry no gwIndicators entries by design, not by omission of effort. All 31 rows below are new Inditex-2022 material; the pilot report covered only Inditex 2024 and H&M 2024, so no pilot rows required folding/deduplication for this assignment.
