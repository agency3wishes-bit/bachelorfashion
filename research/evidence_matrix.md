# Evidence Matrix — Literature Review & Methodology (REBUILT, full 30-source corpus)

Built from all 30 source-note cards present in `research/source_notes/` (S01–S30) and the
block/section map in `resources/approved_source_corpus.md`. This is a full rebuild, not an
incremental edit: every cell below was re-derived directly from the corresponding card's own
DIRECT/PARTIAL/UNVERIFIED tags (or, where a card mixes tags across its "What it says for OUR
topic" bullets, the strongest tag actually attached to the specific claim relevant to that
section). No claim is added beyond what a card states.

**All 30 cards (S01–S30) are present.** No cards are missing in this run.

## Global caveat (applies to every cell below)

Every one of the 30 cards was built **entirely from WebSearch snippets**. In all 30 cards,
every direct WebFetch/full-text retrieval attempt (publisher pages, ResearchGate, Semantic
Scholar, institutional repositories, PMC, RePEc, etc.) returned HTTP 403. No card contains a
single line read directly from a primary PDF/HTML page. This means:
- A card's own "DIRECT" tag means "wording/finding recurred near-identically across
  independent search-engine snippets" — a search-consensus confidence level, **not**
  primary-source verification. Page numbers are absent everywhere.
- "PARTIAL" generally means a plausible paraphrase recurred but wording/attribution is
  unconfirmed, the claim was reconstructed from a single AI-generated summary, or the claim
  is confirmed at a general/field level but not confirmed as *this specific article's own*
  framing.
- "UNVERIFIED" means the claim is either contradicted/ambiguous across snippets, sourced from
  a possibly-conflated adjacent paper, explicitly flagged as a query-echo risk, or simply not
  found despite targeted searching.
Treat every "DIRECT" cell below as "best available, not yet primary-verified." Full-text
access remains an open, task-wide risk noted in every card's Evidence Gaps section.
