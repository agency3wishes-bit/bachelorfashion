# Evidence Matrix — Literature Review & Methodology

Built strictly from the 24 source-note cards present in `research/source_notes/` (S01–S24)
and the block/section map in `resources/approved_source_corpus.md`. No claims are added
beyond what a card states; every tag below is the card's own DIRECT / PARTIAL / UNVERIFIED
label (or, where a card mixes tags across its "What it says for OUR topic" bullets, the
strongest tag actually attached to the specific claim used for that section).

## Global caveat (applies to every cell below)

Every one of the 24 cards was built **entirely from WebSearch snippets**. In all 24 cards,
every direct WebFetch/full-text retrieval attempt (publisher pages, ResearchGate, Semantic
Scholar, institutional repositories, PMC, etc.) returned HTTP 403. No card contains a single
line read directly from a primary PDF/HTML page. This means:
- A card's own "DIRECT" tag means "wording recurred near-identically across independent
  search-engine snippets" — a search-consensus confidence level, **not** primary-source
  verification. Page numbers are absent everywhere; no quote in any card is citable with a
  page number yet.
- "PARTIAL" generally means a plausible paraphrase recurred but wording/attribution is
  unconfirmed, or a claim was reconstructed from a single AI-generated summary.
- "UNVERIFIED" means the claim is either contradicted/ambiguous across snippets, sourced
  from a possibly-conflated adjacent paper, or simply not found.
Treat every "DIRECT" cell below as "best available, not yet primary-verified" — full-text
access remains an open task-wide risk noted in every card's Evidence Gaps section.
